# QurTag — photography brief

The single largest lift available to make this look like Apple or Tumi
instead of a Shopify template is replacing the rendered phone mockup +
black-card placeholders with real editorial photography.

Hire **one** photographer for **two days**. Budget $4–8k including a stylist
and a small location fee. You get every hero asset for the marketing site,
press kit, and gifting collateral in one shoot.

## Vibe references

Look at:

- **Bellroy** — clean product shots on neutral backgrounds, soft side light,
  shallow depth of field.
- **Aesop** — editorial lifestyle, hands, surfaces, no faces.
- **Things 3** — product UI photographed on real desks.
- **Tumi** — hotel rooms, hands on luggage handles, deep blacks.
- **Linear** — gorgeous static product shots paired with calm motion.

Avoid:

- Stock photography. We can spot it instantly and so can the customer.
- Bright saturated color. Stay in the brand palette: bone, ink, verdigris,
  one signal-coral accent (and use that accent sparingly).
- 3D renders. Real materials read differently from rendered ones at the
  hero scale we're using.

## Shot list — must-haves

### 1. Hero composition
A Rimowa Original Cabin on a Hotel Saint Cecilia–style bed, a QurTag
Signature leather tag clipped to the handle. Soft window light. Shot from
hip height, slight foreshortening on the suitcase. Bone-colored linens.

### 2. The app, in hand
A real iPhone (15 Pro or 16 Pro, midnight) held in a woman's hand against a
neutral background, displaying the QurTag item detail screen. The app UI
needs to be designed for the shot — clean state, signal-coral armed badge
visible. The phone takes 60% of frame.

### 3. The tag, in context
Close-up macro of the leather Signature tag — visible stitching, monogram
"C", brass D-ring. Out of focus suitcase handle in background.

### 4. The moment of finding
A woman's hand (no face) picking up the bag from a hotel bench. Tag visible.
Caption-able: "She had your bag at 8:43pm in Lisbon."

### 5. The finder UI on a phone
Same iPhone treatment as shot 2, but showing the finder page in Portuguese,
with the share-location button highlighted. Communicates translation-by-
default without saying it.

### 6. The print-at-home moment
Hands holding a freshly printed QurTag tag, scissors and a luggage sleeve
visible at the edge of frame. Communicates "this isn't precious or
expensive — it's accessible."

### 7. The four hardware tiers, lined up
Studio-lit, top-down. Core silicone in matte black, Pro anodized aluminum,
Signature leather with brass, Track aluminum with subtle BLE indicator.
This is the configurator hero.

### 8. The dropoff at a partner
A hand sliding a tagged bag across a hotel front desk. Concierge in soft
focus. Brand-neutral hotel — we'll do partner-specific shoots later.

## Style and post

- 35mm equivalent for hero compositions, 90mm macro for product close-ups.
- Natural light wherever possible. Reflectors over flash.
- Color grade: lift the shadows slightly, hold whites in bone tone, never
  let the blacks go true 0 — keep texture in the deep shadows.
- Output: 4K + 2K + low-res JPG for every shot. Plus a square crop and a
  16:9 hero crop for each.
